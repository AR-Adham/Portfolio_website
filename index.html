About Me

I am a Mechanical Engineering student at Queen’s University with a strong foundation in both practical industry experience and technical design. Over the past few years, I’ve completed four internships across diverse industries, gaining hands-on exposure to automation, manufacturing, and machinery. At Checkpoint Group in Toronto, I contributed to the design and implementation of automated storage and retrieval systems (ASRS) and end-of-line automation solutions, while also working on more traditional racking projects for warehouses. In Dubai, I supported Technopower in launching a new business focused on extending the life of dies used in pellet manufacturing. I also trained with Nestlé Dubai Manufacturing, where I gained valuable insight into large-scale factory operations and helped refine preventive maintenance plans. Most recently, I interned with Caterpillar Al-Bahar in Sharjah, rotating across divisions to learn how heavy equipment operates while developing structured maintenance plans for various machines.

My passion lies at the intersection of robotics, mechanical design, and coding. I’ve been deeply involved in robotics since high school, where I competed in the FIRST Robotics Competition with Team 5409. As Design Lead, Scouting Lead, and later Team Captain, I played a central role in designing our competition robots while helping lead a team that consistently ranked among top contenders. I later returned as a mentor, sharing my design and leadership experience with new members. Today, I continue to build on that foundation—driven by a curiosity for how things work, a commitment to problem-solving through engineering, and a motivation to design innovative systems that bring together mechanics, automation, and software.
